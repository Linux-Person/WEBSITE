About

About the Home Hosting Network.

Home Hosting Network was kinda started by accident. I the owner have always messed around with server stuff and over the years have been hosting servers for me and my friends. Then one random night I came up with the Home Hosting Network name and made the logo in about 10 minutes.

At first it was just a normal survival server, no griefing, no anarchy, just a chill server. The problem was I had online mode set to false so my friends who didn't pay for the game could join. That was a bad idea. The server had around 15 people and got absolutely destroyed. It was quite the experience.

I shut the server down for about 4 or 5 days and rethought everything. The server went through a semi-anarchy phase for a while — griefing allowed, cheats allowed, world never reset. Now we've gone back to something more special: Beta 1.7.3. The original Minecraft experience, before everything got complicated.

Why do I host it? I just like servers. I like the idea of anyone being able to join and leave their mark on the world. Beta Minecraft is a special version of the game — the world generation, the atmosphere, the simplicity. That's really it.
